Custom-built ECG with Signal Processing

Spring 2024

Course: EECE2150 - Circuits and Signals: Biomedical Applications

Developed a complete electrocardiogram system from components:

  • Designed and built custom circuitry using AD627 and LT1490 operational amplifiers
  • Implemented cascaded high-pass and low-pass filters for signal conditioning
  • Applied careful circuit layout and shielding techniques to minimize noise
  • Digitized ECG signals through National Instruments DAQ hardware
  • Processed signals in MATLAB to remove 60 Hz noise using Parks-McClellan and notch filters
  • Performed frequency domain analysis through Fourier Transform
  • Developed algorithms to extract cardiac parameters and calculate heart rate

This project combined analog circuit design, digital signal processing, and biomedical instrumentation principles to create a functional medical device, demonstrating the intersection of engineering and healthcare applications.
